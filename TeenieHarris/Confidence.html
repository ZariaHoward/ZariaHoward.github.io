The confidence in the results in analyzing composition in photo heavily depends on how much confidence we can have in the subjects of the photo that we picked up. The facial recognition library that was used to detect faces in the library gives a confidence metric which tells us the probability that the pixels detected are actually a human face. The face sizes retrieved from the library are fixed and discrete.

The majority of the faces detected are small, this is most likely an artifact of the fact that most of Teenie's photos are group pictures and crowd shots. The more people he fit into a photo, the smaller everyone's faces were. However in his portrait photographs with one or two people the faces seemed to be significantly larger. Faces that are bigger in the photo almost always have a higher confidence associated with them. Faces that are smaller have a confidences anywhere from 0 to 1.2, the variance is greater in small faces.

As you can see in the chart above, it was rare to have a large face and a low confidence.
